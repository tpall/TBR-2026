# TBR 2026 — Pre-Race Strength, Taper & Activation

**Generated:** 2026-05-24
**Event:** Trans Balkan Race 2026
**Race start:** Fri 29 May 2026, 09:00 CEST, Trieste

This block carries you from Sunday gym work through race-morning activation. The principle: prime the system, open the hips and T-spine, never induce DOMS.

---

## Day 1 — Sun 24 May: Mobility & Activation (≈55 min)

**Goal:** open the saddle-specific tissues (hips, T-spine, hip flexors), light glute/core activation. Zero loading.

**Warm-up (10 min)**
- 5 min easy spin / row / elliptical, Z1, nose breathing
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5/side
- Cat-cow — 10
- Hip CARs (controlled circles) — 5/side

**Mobility block (15 min)**
- 90/90 hip switches — 3 × 8/side
- Couch stretch — 2 × 45s/side
- Thread-the-needle — 2 × 8/side
- Open-book T-spine rotation — 2 × 10/side
- Banded shoulder pass-throughs — 2 × 12

**Activation (20 min) — bodyweight or light band only**
- Glute bridges — 3 × 12
- Bird-dog (slow tempo) — 3 × 8/side
- Side plank — 3 × 30s/side
- Dead bug — 3 × 10/side
- Banded clamshells — 3 × 15/side
- Wall slides — 3 × 10

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Foam roll: quads, IT band, glutes, T-spine
- 2 min diaphragmatic breathing, legs up the wall

## Day 2 — Mon 25 May: Functional Priming (≈60 min)

**Goal:** stimulate posterior chain and pulling muscles (climbing + loaded bike), low load, stop every set 2–3 reps shy of failure.

**Warm-up (10 min)**
- 5 min easy cardio
- Inchworm walkouts — 8
- Reverse lunge + rotation — 6/side
- A-skips — 20 m × 2

**Strength priming (30 min) — RPE 5–6 max, no grinding, no slow eccentrics**
- Goblet squat — 3 × 8, light
- Romanian deadlift (DB or KB) — 3 × 8, light
- Single-leg glute bridge — 3 × 10/side
- Half-kneeling cable/band row — 3 × 10/side
- Push-ups (knees fine) — 3 × 8–10
- Pallof press — 3 × 10/side

**Saddle-specific mobility (15 min)**
- Pigeon stretch — 2 × 60s/side
- Couch stretch — 2 × 60s/side
- Foam roll quads — 2 min/side
- Upper trap / levator scapulae stretch — 2 min (matters more than you think on day 5)
- Wrist & forearm mobility — 2 min

**Cool-down (5 min):** supine, legs up the wall, slow nasal breathing.

---

## Day 3 — Tue 26 May: Short Openers (≈40 min)

**Goal:** wake the legs, sharpen the nervous system, get out. Lightest day. Travel is tomorrow.

**Warm-up (10 min)**
- 5 min very easy cardio
- Leg swings, arm circles, hip openers — 5 min

**Openers (15 min)**
- Bodyweight squats — 2 × 10
- Banded lateral walks — 2 × 10 steps/side
- Low-box step-ups, bodyweight — 2 × 8/side
- **Bike openers** (preferred over treadmill): **3 × 30s seated at 180–200 W**, with 90s easy spin between

Stop. That's the work.

**Recovery / mobility (15 min)**
- Full-body foam roll
- Couch stretch, pigeon, hamstring stretch — 45s/side each
- Box breathing 4-4-4-4 for 3 min

## Day 4 — Wed 27 May: Travel day (LJU → Trieste)

No gym. Move when you can.

- 10 min mobility before the bus: hip flexor stretch, T-spine rotations, neck circles
- On the bus: stand up / walk the aisle at every chance; calf raises while seated; ankle circles
- Hotel arrival: 5 min easy bodyweight squats + lunges to flush the legs
- Eat well, hydrate aggressively, lights out by 22:30

---

## Day 5 — Thu 28 May: Pre-Race Shakeout in Trieste (≈30 min)

**Goal:** confirm bike is race-ready, light spin with sharp openers, zero fatigue.

**Bike check first (do this before you ride):**
- Drivetrain: shifts cleanly across cassette, chain not skipping
- Brakes: pads not contacting, rotors true
- Tubeless: tyre pressure dialled (~3.5–4.5 bar depending on load)
- Lights: front + rear fully charged, mounted
- GPS: route loaded, batteries topped
- Bolts: stem, seatpost, cleats — all torqued
- Bags: dry-bag straps tight, no rattle

**Shakeout ride (20 min)**
- 10 min easy Z1 spin on a quiet road
- **3 × 30 sec at race effort + 3–4 hard pedal strokes out of saddle** (~250–280 W) with 2 min easy between — wakes the type-II fibres
- 5 min Z1 spin back

**Return mobility (10 min)**
- Couch stretch — 2 × 45s/side
- Pigeon — 2 × 45s/side
- T-spine rotations — 10/side
- Neck and shoulder rolls

**Then:** lay out kit, pin number on, prep bottles + first 3 h of food for the morning, in bed by 21:30. Treat tomorrow's start as a long day at the office — nothing more.

## Race Morning — Fri 29 May: Activation (≈15 min)

Start 09:00. Suggested timeline:

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| 05:30 | Wake. Coffee. |
| 06:00 | Breakfast (eat ≥3 h before start) — porridge + banana + honey, plus an espresso if tolerated |
| 07:00 | Bag check, kit on, lights and GPS double-check |
| 07:45 | Roll to start area, easy spin |
| 08:00 | Mobility activation (hotel or quiet corner near start) |
| 08:30 | Final bottle top-up, last gel in pocket, breathing |
| 08:50 | At start line |
| **09:00** | **GO** |

**Activation routine (~10 min)**
- Hip CARs — 3/side
- Cossack squats — 5/side
- World's Greatest Stretch — 3/side
- 90/90 hip switches — 5/side
- Glute bridges (bodyweight) — 12
- Banded glute walks if you packed a mini-band — 10 steps/side
- A-skips or high knees — 20 m × 2 (elevate HR)
- Shoulder rolls + neck circles
- 5 slow diaphragmatic breaths (4 sec in, 6 sec out)

**On the bike, pre-start (~5 min)**
- Easy Z1 spin around the start area
- 2 × 10 sec brisk pedalling out of saddle to check shoes/cleats
- Settle in, eyes up, race face

## Rules of thumb for the whole block

- **No new exercises.** If something here is unfamiliar, swap it for a movement pattern you know.
- **Stop sets early.** RPE 5–6 ceiling. If a rep feels grindy, rack it.
- **Skip if sore.** Anything sore the next morning → drop to mobility-only.
- **Hydrate + carb up.** These sessions burn very little; don't under-fuel.
- **Sleep is the workout this week.** 8+ hours every night. Lights out before 23:00.
