# Trans Balkan Race 2026 — Preliminary Weather Outlook

> **Based on climatological normals (1991–2020), not a live forecast.**
> Check actual forecasts 7–10 days before race start at:
> meteo.hr (Croatia), hidmet.gov.ba (BiH), meteo.co.me (Montenegro), yr.no (all regions)

## Route climate zones

The route crosses 5 distinct climate zones in 7 days:

| Zone | Route section | Elevation | Climate type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Adriatic coast/Karst | Trieste → Slavnik (km 0–28) | 0–1000 m | Mediterranean / sub-Mediterranean |
| 2. Dinaric mountains | Platak → Velebit → Gospic (km 141–345) | 700–1600 m | Mountain, very wet (Gorski Kotar) |
| 3. Continental inland | Knin → Livno → Mostar (km 537–812) | 60–700 m | Continental / Mediterranean transition |
| 4. Bosnian highlands | Nevesinje → Sutjeska → Kopilovi (km 858–1060) | 400–1200 m | Continental-mountain, wet |
| 5. Montenegro mountains → coast | Durmitor → Niksic → Risan (km 1060–1395) | 0–1450 m | Mountain → Mediterranean |

## Day-by-day outlook

### Day 1 — Fri 29 May: Trieste → km 179
**Route: Trieste → Karst → Slavnik (1028m) → Croatian border → Platak (1100m) → Fuzine → bivvy**
**Riding: 09:00–00:30 · Night section above 700m**

| Parameter | Daytime (09:00–20:00) | Evening/Night (20:00–00:30) |
|---|---|---|
| **Terrain** | Coast → Karst ridges → Gorski Kotar mountains | Mountain descent → Fuzine valley |
| **Temperature** | 22–25 °C coast, dropping to 16–19 °C above 1000m | 9–12 °C at altitude, 12–14 °C in Fuzine valley |
| **Precipitation** | 25–35% chance; afternoon thunderstorms possible over Slavnik and Platak | Storms can persist into evening in mountains |
| **Wind** | Possible Bora (NE) on Karst above Trieste; sea breeze on coast | Mountain drainage winds, generally light |
| **Key hazard** | Afternoon thunderstorms on exposed Slavnik ridge; lightning risk | Cold descent from Platak in dark; wet roads |

**Gear notes:** Start light (short sleeves). Pack arm warmers + wind vest accessible for Slavnik climb. Rain jacket mandatory for Gorski Kotar. Night section at altitude will feel cold (wind chill ~5–7 °C on descent).

**Expected conditions: Warm start, cooling steadily. 1 in 3 chance of thunderstorms over mountains.**

### Day 2 — Sat 30 May: km 179 → km 409
**Route: Gorski Kotar → Oltari → Velebit/Zavisan (1594m) → Lika basin → Gospic → bivvy past Gospic**
**Riding: 06:30–01:59 · Highest point of entire race (Zavisan)**

| Parameter | Morning (06:30–12:00) | Afternoon (12:00–20:00) | Night (20:00–01:59) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Terrain** | Climb through Gorski Kotar forest → Velebit ascent | Velebit summit/descent → Lika plateau | Lika plateau riding |
| **Temperature** | 10–14 °C start (mountain); **2–8 °C at Zavisan summit** | 8–12 °C summit; 19–23 °C Gospic | 7–10 °C in Lika basin |
| **Precipitation** | 35–40% morning fog/drizzle in Gorski Kotar | **50–60% at Zavisan** — afternoon storms very likely | Storms typically end by late evening |
| **Wind** | Light in forest; **strong at Zavisan — Bora gusts 60–100 km/h possible** | Exposed ridgeline dangerously windy in storms | Calm in Lika basin |
| **Key hazard** | **Zavisan is the most dangerous weather point on the route.** Hypothermia risk from cold + wind + rain at 1600m. Frost still possible in late May. | Lightning on exposed Velebit ridge | Cold bivvy in Lika (7 °C) |

**Gear notes:** Full rain jacket + thermal base layer for Velebit crossing. Gloves recommended. This is the day where bad weather can end your race — if a storm hits on Velebit, shelter in Dom Zavisan hut until it passes. Gospic will feel warm by comparison.

**Expected conditions: Cold mountain start, potentially harsh at Velebit summit. Warm relief in Lika. Cold night.**

### Day 4 — Mon 1 Jun: km 646 → km 882
**Route: Livno plateau → Suica → descend to Mostar → climb to Nevesinje → bivvy**
**Riding: 06:30–01:59**

| Parameter | Morning (06:30–12:00) | Afternoon (12:00–20:00) | Night (20:00–01:59) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Terrain** | Livno plateau (700m) → mountain crossing → Mostar approach | **Descent into Neretva valley / Mostar (60m)** | Climb from Mostar to Nevesinje (900m) |
| **Temperature** | 12–16 °C (Livno plateau); warming on descent | **27–32 °C in Mostar** — hottest point on entire route | 19–22 °C leaving Mostar; dropping to 9–12 °C at Nevesinje |
| **Precipitation** | 35–40% (Livno plateau showers) | 25–30% (Mostar drier) | 40% (mountain convection) |
| **Wind** | Light on plateau | Valley thermals in Neretva canyon; generally light | Light |
| **Key hazard** | — | **HEAT.** Mostar is notorious for heat — 30+ °C in late May is normal, 35+ °C possible. Neretva valley radiates heat off limestone. Dehydration risk extreme | **20 °C temperature drop** climbing from Mostar to Nevesinje in 46 km |

**Gear notes:** Strip down for Mostar (short sleeves, maximum hydration). Carry 1.5–2L water leaving Mostar. Then layer back up immediately on the Nevesinje climb — the thermal shock is the biggest single-ride temperature swing on the route (~20 °C).

**Expected conditions: Cool morning, VERY hot in Mostar, dramatic cooling into night. Highest heat risk of the race.**

## Key weather hazards ranked

1. **Hypothermia at Velebit/Zavisan (Day 2)** — 2–8 °C + Bora gusts + rain at 1600m. Shelter at Dom Zavisan if conditions are severe.
2. **Heat stroke at Mostar (Day 4)** — 30–35 °C in the Neretva valley. Hydrate aggressively, seek shade if needed.
3. **Hypothermia at Durmitor (Day 6)** — 3–6 °C + exposed ridgeline. Similar to Velebit but you're 5 days more fatigued.
4. **Thunderstorms/lightning (Days 2, 5, 6)** — Exposed mountain ridges in afternoon. Avoid being the highest point.
5. **Night riding in wet, cold conditions (any day)** — Wet kit + wind chill + fatigue + altitude = dangerous combination.
