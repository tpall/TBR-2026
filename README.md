## Key Warnings

### Landmines
Real danger zones — **never leave the GPX track**:

| Area | km | Type |
|---|---|---|
| Velebit (CRO) | 155–338 | Warning zone |
| Dinara (CRO/BiH) | 540–648 | **Danger zone** |
| Cincar-Tomislavgrad (BiH) | 669–696 | Warning zone |
| Šuica-Tomislavgrad (BiH) | 702–749 | **Danger zone** |

In danger zones: no detours, avoid riding at night, camp on the trail itself if needed.

### GPX Navigation
Use only the official file loaded directly onto your device. **Do NOT use Komoot, RideWithGPS, or similar apps** — they auto-reroute and can send you into mined areas.

### Other Hazards
- **Water:** Karst terrain is dry. Carry water purification.
- **Bura wind:** Up to 120–220 km/h on Velebit — potentially unrideable.
- **Heat:** Up to 40–43°C on exposed sections (Dinara, Crkvičko Polje). Temperatures swing to near freezing at night.
- **Electric fences:** Unmarked, nearly invisible at speed — especially before CP1. Slow down on fast descents.
- **Wildlife:** Shepherd dogs (stop, don't flee), bears, wolves, wild boar, free-roaming horses, deer.

### Tough Sections
- **Velebit** — extreme Bura wind exposure
- **Dinara** — dry, exposed, 40°C+, Bura risk
- **Cincar to Široki Brijeg** — scarce water, careful planning needed
- **Sutjeska (Ulog → Popov Most)** — mud/loose gravel after rain, may need to push
- **Crkvičko Polje** — no resupply, arid karst plateau
- **Sinjajevina/Pandurica (Žabljak → Nikšić)** — deteriorated mountain trail, likely hike-a-bike sections

## Rules

8 golden rules:
1. Cover the entire route by bike; validate brevet card at both checkpoints within cut-offs
2. Complete in total self-sufficiency — no external support
3. Solo or Pair only; max 10% of route ridden alongside others
4. Carry mandatory equipment throughout
5. Maintain evidence of your ride
6. Observe local and traffic laws
7. No littering — immediate disqualification if caught
8. Race Director's decision is final

## Mandatory Equipment

- Helmet
- Front and rear lights
- First aid kit
- Survival blanket
- Insulated jacket (down or synthetic)

Recommended: tick kit, warm gloves, water purification, sleeping system, waterproof jacket and pants.

## Mandatory Documents

- Valid ID card (EU) or passport
- Medical certificate (states fitness to participate)
- Travel insurance covering search & rescue and repatriation
- Signed liability waiver

Submit documents via email to hello@transbalkanrace.com before the race, or bring to accreditation.

## Logistics

### Luggage Transfer
One bike box or small bag transferred Trieste → Risan. Hand in at accreditation on May 28 — **truck leaves 20:00, nothing collected on May 29**.

### Tracking
SPOT GEN4 devices available for rent. €200 cash deposit at accreditation, refunded at finish upon tracker return.

### Money
- EUR: Slovenia, Croatia, Montenegro
- BAM: Bosnia & Herzegovina (euros accepted, change given in BAM)
- Carry cash — cards rarely work in rural areas

### SIM Cards
- EU roaming works in Slovenia and Croatia
- Buy local SIMs in BiH (~€10, **hej** operator, ~20 GB) and Montenegro (~€10, **m:tel**, ~400 GB)
- Passport or ID required to buy SIM in Montenegro

### Getting to the Start (Trieste)
- Trieste Airport: 30 min by train (~€5)
- Ljubljana Airport: direct trains several times/day (~€9)
- Venice Airport also nearby

### Getting Home from Kotor Bay
- Tivat Airport: 14 km
- Podgorica Airport: 80 km (more connections)
- Ferry (Jadrolinja) and bus options available
